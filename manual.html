Earthquake

Before an Earthquake:

- Secure heavy items and furniture to prevent them from toppling.

- Create an emergency kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, and first aid supplies.

- Develop a family communication plan and identify safe spots in your home.

During an Earthquake:

- Drop, cover, and hold on. Take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture.

- Stay indoors if you are inside a building, and avoid doorways or windows.

- If you are outside, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, streetlights, and utility wires.

After an Earthquake:

- Check for injuries and administer first aid.

- Assess your surroundings for hazards like gas leaks or downed power lines.

- Listen to the radio for updates and instructions from local authorities.

Hurricane

Before a Hurricane:

- Create an evacuation plan and identify evacuation routes.

- Stock up on non-perishable food, water, medications, and essential supplies.

- Protect windows and doors with storm shutters or plywood.

During a Hurricane:

- Stay indoors and away from windows.

- Be prepared to take shelter in a small interior room if necessary.

- Avoid using candles and be cautious of flooding.

After a Hurricane:

- Listen to local news and follow instructions from emergency services.

- Avoid floodwaters and downed power lines.

- Check your home for damage and use caution when cleaning up.

Flood

Before a Flood:

- Know your area's flood risk and prepare an emergency kit.

- Move important items to higher ground and consider flood insurance.

- Create an emergency communication plan with family members.

During a Flood:

- Evacuate if instructed to do so and move to higher ground.

- Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.

- Stay informed through local news and weather updates.

After a Flood:

- Return home only when authorities say it is safe.

- Check for structural damage before re-entering your home.

- Be cautious of contaminated water and potential hazards.

Tornado

Before a Tornado:

- Have a tornado emergency plan for your family.

- Create a tornado survival kit with essentials like a flashlight, batteries, and a weather radio.

- Identify a safe shelter location, such as a basement or an interior room on the lowest floor.

During a Tornado:

- Seek shelter immediately in the designated safe location. If you're in a vehicle, abandon it and find a sturdy building or lie flat in a ditch.

- Protect yourself from flying debris by covering your head and neck with your arms or a heavy blanket. Stay away from windows.

- Listen to weather alerts and updates for information on the tornado's path and duration.

After a Tornado:

- Check for injuries and provide first aid to those in need. Contact emergency services if necessary.

- Be cautious of downed power lines, broken glass, and debris when moving around. Watch out for potential hazards.

- Help your neighbors and community with recovery efforts, if safe to do so.

Wildfire

Before a Wildfire:

- Create a defensible space around your home by clearing away dry vegetation and debris.

- Develop an evacuation plan and make sure family members know where to meet in case of separation.

- Pack an emergency kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, and protective clothing.

During a Wildfire:

- Evacuate immediately if instructed to do so. Follow designated evacuation routes.

- Stay informed about the wildfire's progress through local news and weather updates.

- Protect yourself from smoke and flying embers with a mask and appropriate clothing.

After a Wildfire:

- Wait for authorities to declare it safe to return to your home and follow their instructions.

- Be cautious of damaged infrastructure, downed power lines, and unstable terrain when surveying the area.

- Support recovery efforts in your community and help those in need.

Tsunami

Before a Tsunami:

- Identify tsunami evacuation routes and safe locations on higher ground.

- Create a family emergency plan, including a communication strategy in case of separation.

- Assemble an emergency kit with water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, and a portable weather radio.

During a Tsunami:

- Evacuate immediately if you receive a tsunami warning. Follow designated routes to higher ground.

- Move inland and to higher ground, and avoid low-lying coastal areas and beaches.

- Stay tuned to weather alerts and local broadcasts for updates on the tsunami's progress.

After a Tsunami:

- Wait for authorities to declare it safe to return to coastal areas and heed their guidance.

- Be aware of potential aftershocks and secondary hazards like flooding and debris in the aftermath.

- Assist with search and rescue efforts, if you are trained and it's safe to do so.

Volcano Eruption

Before a Volcano Eruption:

- Familiarize yourself with local volcano monitoring and emergency information sources.

- Prepare an emergency kit with face masks, goggles, and respiratory protection in case of ashfall.

- Develop a family communication plan and establish meeting points if you need to evacuate.

During a Volcano Eruption:

- Stay informed about volcanic activity through local authorities and official sources.

- Follow evacuation orders and move to designated safe areas if required.

- Protect yourself from volcanic ash by wearing appropriate clothing and respiratory gear.

After a Volcano Eruption:

- Wait for authorities to declare it safe to return to affected areas and follow their guidance.

- Avoid ash-contaminated water and be cautious of ash-related hazards such as roof collapse.

- Assist with disaster response and recovery efforts, if you are able and it's safe to do so.

Landslide

Before a Landslide:

- Be aware of the warning signs, such as ground cracks, sudden increase or decrease in water flow, and tilting trees.

- Avoid building on steep slopes or in areas with a history of landslides.

- Create an emergency kit with essential supplies and important documents.

During a Landslide:

- If you suspect a landslide, leave the area immediately and warn others.

- Avoid rivers and low-lying areas, as landslides can trigger floods.

- Stay indoors if evacuation is not possible.

After a Landslide:

- Avoid the affected area until authorities declare it safe.

- Be cautious of secondary landslides and flooding.

- Listen to local news and follow instructions from emergency services.

Drought

Before a Drought:

- Create a water conservation plan, including reducing water usage and fixing leaks in your home.

- Stockpile essential supplies, especially water and non-perishable food items.

- Develop an emergency communication plan and stay informed about drought conditions in your area.

During a Drought:

- Use water sparingly and follow local water use restrictions or guidelines in place.

- Conserve water by collecting rainwater, fixing leaks, and practicing water-efficient habits.

- Stay informed about drought forecasts and be prepared for potential impacts on agriculture and water supply.

After a Drought:

- Continue water conservation efforts even after the drought ends to maintain sustainable water use practices.

- Support local agriculture and communities affected by the drought through relief efforts and aid programs.

- Plan for future droughts by implementing long-term water conservation strategies and preparedness measures.

Avalanche

Before an Avalanche:

- Check avalanche forecasts and conditions in your area.

- Inform others about your plans and estimated return time.

- Carry essential avalanche safety equipment, including a beacon, shovel, and probe.

During an Avalanche:

- Try to move to the side to escape the flow of the avalanche.

- Swim in the direction of the surface if you're buried.

- Create an air pocket to breathe if buried.

After an Avalanche:

- Signal for help if you're buried.

- Provide aid to others if possible.

- Report the incident to authorities and seek medical attention.
